## Chronological Flow of Key Doctrines

### 75 AD - Deuterocanon Already Scripture
Barnabas quotes Wisdom as authoritative

### 80 AD - Rome Exercises Authority
Pope Clement commands Corinth to obey

### 107 AD - "Catholic Church" First Named
Ignatius uses the term twice in letters

### 130 AD - Papal Succession Documented
Hegesippus travels to Rome, lists popes

### 155 AD - Real Presence Affirmed
Justin Martyr: "Not common bread... but the flesh and blood of Jesus"

### 155 AD - Mary as New Eve
Justin: Eve brought death, Mary brought salvation

### 170 AD - Rome's Charitable Primacy
Dionysius praises Rome's historic care for all churches

### 180 AD - Comprehensive Apologetic
Irenaeus synthesizes: Succession, Primacy, Catholic Church, Mary, Deuterocanon

### 200 AD - Catholic Church's Antiquity
Clement of Alexandria: "The ancient and Catholic Church is alone"

## Apologetic Value

This timeline devastates common Protestant objections:

1. **"Sola Scriptura"** ‚Üí Fathers cite deuterocanon as Scripture
2. **"Bible alone determines canon"** ‚Üí Church determined canon through succession
3. **"No papal primacy"** ‚Üí Rome exercises authority from 80 AD
4. **"Catholic Church invented later"** ‚Üí Named in 107 AD
5. **"Mary worship is medieval"** ‚Üí Mary as New Eve in 155 AD
6. **"Tradition contradicts Scripture"** ‚Üí Fathers see them as unified
7. **"Democracy in early Church"** ‚Üí Monarchical bishops from beginning
